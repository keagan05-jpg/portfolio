Why Chelsea

Football was always the plan

"Matchday content lives or dies in seconds. I've been studying that problem as a fan for years — now I want to solve it from the inside."

Football wasn't a career pivot. It's where I first started paying attention to social media — watching how clubs handle a 90th-minute winner, a controversial VAR call, a transfer deadline day. The best teams don't just cover what happens. They have a voice, a pace, a way of making fans feel included even when they're watching from their phone.

Chelsea's digital output is one of the best in the Premier League to study. The TikTok channel has genuine personality. The matchday content on X moves fast without losing the brand. I want to be part of building that — not just as a fan, but as someone who knows how to publish, edit and optimise content that performs.

What I've noticed — and what I'd bring

Three observations from watching Chelsea's channels as both a fan and a content professional.

Opportunity

TikTok personality vs. cross-platform consistency

Chelsea's TikTok has more personality than the Instagram feed. There's room to bring more of that energy into Reels and Stories without losing the brand — the tone gap between platforms is wider than it needs to be.

Strength

Reactive content on X is genuinely good

The speed of Chelsea's X account on matchday is impressive — the right meme, the right clip, the right caption at the right moment. That instinct is hard to teach. I've been watching closely to understand how they make those calls under pressure.

Idea

Fan data capture through content, not ads

The clubs doing fan sign-ups best aren't running banners — they're building formats (polls, prediction games, WhatsApp drops) that fans actually want to engage with. I'd want to contribute ideas in that space from day one.
